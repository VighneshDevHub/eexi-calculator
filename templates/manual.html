1. Understanding EEXI

Energy Efficiency Existing Ship Index

The EEXI is a technical measure introduced by the IMO to reduce GHG emissions from ships. Unlike the EEDI (for new builds), EEXI applies to existing ships above 400 GT. It evaluates the vessel's technical design efficiency under standardized conditions.

Key Goal

Reduce carbon intensity by at least 40% by 2030 across international shipping.

Applicability

Applies to major vessel categories including Bulk Carriers, Tankers, and Container Ships.

2. Understanding CII

Carbon Intensity Indicator

The CII is an operational measure. While EEXI looks at design, CII looks at how you actually sail. It rates ships from A (Superior) to E (Inferior) based on annual operational efficiency.

Operational

Based on actual annual fuel consumption and distance.

Annual

Calculated yearly with stricter targets every year.

Rating

D or E ratings require an updated SEEMP Plan.

3. Calculation Methodology

IMO Regulatory Formulas

EEXI Formula
Attained EEXI = (PME × CF × SFC + PAE × CF × SFC) / (fi × Capacity × Vref × fw)

Technical Terms

  • PME: Main Engine power at 75% MCR.
  • CF: Carbon conversion factor (Fuel specific).
  • SFC: Specific Fuel Consumption (Certified).

Correction Factors

  • fi: Capacity correction factor.
  • fw: Weather correction factor.
  • fc: Cubic capacity factor.

5. Compliance & Solutions

What to do if non-compliant

Non-Compliant?

If your attained index is higher than required, you must take technical action.

Engine Power Limitation (EPL): Our tool automatically calculates the required power reduction to bring your ship back into compliance.

Best Practices

  • Regular hull and propeller cleaning.
  • Route optimization and slow steaming.
  • Investment in energy-saving devices.
