1. Introduction to EEXI

The Energy Efficiency Existing Ship Index (EEXI) is a mandatory technical measure introduced by the International Maritime Organization (IMO) under MARPOL Annex VI. It applies to existing ships above 400 GT to ensure they meet minimum energy efficiency standards.

Goal: Reduce the carbon intensity of international shipping by 40% by 2030.

Scope: Bulk carriers, tankers, container ships, cruise ships, and more.

2. Mathematical Methodology

The application implements the official IMO EEXI calculation formula to determine the CO2 emissions per transport work.

Attained EEXI = (P_ME × C_F_ME × SFC_ME + P_AE × C_F_AE × SFC_AE) / (f_i × Capacity × V_ref × f_w)

3. Step-by-Step Workflow

1 Input Vessel Data

Select your ship type accurately. The tool uses this to pull specific IMO reference coefficients ($a$ and $c$).

2 Define Engine Specs

Provide MCR and SFC from the Engine International Air Pollution Prevention (EIAPP) certificate.

3 Review Compliance

The system compares Attained vs. Required EEXI and provides a detailed compliance margin.

4. Compliance & EPL

If your vessel is non-compliant, the tool automatically calculates the Engine Power Limitation (EPL) required to meet the target.

5. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the difference between EEDI and EEXI?

EEDI is for new ships, while EEXI is for existing ships. Both use similar technical formulas but have different implementation timelines.

Q: Can I use this for official class certification?

This tool is for preliminary assessment. For official certification, please consult with a recognized organization (RO) or class society.
