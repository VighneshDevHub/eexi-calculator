User Manual & Technical Guide

A comprehensive guide to understanding EEXI regulations and using this calculator.

1. Introduction to EEXI

The Energy Efficiency Existing Ship Index (EEXI) is a mandatory technical measure introduced by the IMO to reduce the carbon intensity of international shipping. It applies to all existing ships above 400 GT falling under MARPOL Annex VI.

This tool calculates the Attained EEXI based on your vessel's actual performance and compares it against the Required EEXI (the regulatory limit).

2. Mathematical Methodology

Attained EEXI Formula:

Attained EEXI = (P_ME * C_F_ME * SFC_ME + P_AE * C_F_AE * SFC_AE) / (f_i * Capacity * V_ref * f_w)
  • P_ME: 75% of Main Engine MCR.
  • C_F: CO2 conversion factor based on fuel type.
  • SFC: Specific Fuel Consumption (g/kWh).
  • Capacity: DWT for most, 70% DWT for containers, GT for cruise ships.
  • V_ref: Service speed at 75% MCR.

3. How to Perform a Calculation

  1. Vessel Particulars: Select your ship type carefully; this determines the reference line parameters (a, c) and reduction factor (X).
  2. Engine Data: Enter the MCR and SFC from your engine's technical file.
  3. Auxiliary Data: If your vessel has auxiliary engines, enter their power and consumption for a more accurate assessment.
  4. Correction Factors: Use 1.0 unless you have specific certified values for energy-saving devices.

4. Understanding Results

COMPLIANT

The vessel meets the IMO requirement. The positive margin indicates safety headroom.

BORDERLINE

Compliant, but within a 5% margin. Efficiency improvements may be needed in the future.

NON-COMPLIANT

Fails to meet the requirement. Engine Power Limitation (EPL) is usually required.

5. Engine Power Limitation (EPL)

If your vessel is non-compliant, the system automatically calculates the Limited MCR. This is the maximum power setting allowed to bring the vessel into compliance without structural modifications.

6. Technical Reports

Every calculation can be exported as a professional PDF report. This report includes all input values, the mathematical derivation steps, and the final compliance verdict, suitable for submission to class or authorities.
