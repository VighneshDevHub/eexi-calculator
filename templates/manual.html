User Manual & Technical Guide

A comprehensive guide to understanding EEXI regulations and utilizing the calculator effectively.

1. Introduction to EEXI

The Energy Efficiency Existing Ship Index (EEXI) is a mandatory technical measure introduced by the International Maritime Organization (IMO) to reduce the carbon footprint of international shipping. It applies to existing ships above 400 GT falling under MARPOL Annex VI.

This application calculates the Attained EEXI based on your vessel's actual technical data and compares it against the Required EEXI reference lines established by the IMO.

2. Mathematical Methodology

Attained EEXI = (P_ME × C_F_ME × SFC_ME + P_AE × C_F_AE × SFC_AE) / (f_i × Capacity × V_ref × f_w)

3. Calculation Workflow

Input Data

Select ship type, enter DWT/GT, engine power, fuel type, and design speed.

Verify Status

The system instantly verifies compliance against IMO reference lines.

Get EPL

For non-compliant ships, receive an automated Engine Power Limitation target.

4. Compliance Interpretation

COMPLIANT

Vessel meets requirements. The margin shows the efficiency headroom.

BORDERLINE

Compliant but within 5% of the limit. Optimization is recommended.

NON-COMPLIANT

Vessel fails the requirement. Engine Power Limitation (EPL) is mandatory.
